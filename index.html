— 01 / The Place

A retreat where the forest does the talking.

Camp Bughaw sits on a forested ridge overlooking a mirror-still highland lake in the Philippine cordillera. Bughaw — the old Tagalog word for the deep blue of distant mountains and clear sky — is what you’ll see every morning.

We keep things intentionally small: a handful of canvas tents, a few hardwood cottages, fire pits, hammocks, and a wood-fired sauna. No televisions, no rush. Just trails, lake water, slow meals, and stars you’d forgotten existed.
